Case Study:
GROWING START-UP LOOKS TO standardize FILE SYNC & SHARE SYSTEM practices

The following is from an on-site consulting project at Floyd Inc., an e-commerce start-up based in Detroit.
In my role as Project Manager I worked alongside 3 other graduate students representing the University of Michigan.

1
RESEARCH QUESTIONS


How does use of the current File Sync & Share (FSS) System affect employee morale, productivity, and workflow?


What best practices can we adopt to suit our growing company's needs and address employee concerns?



2
BACKGROUND RESEARCH

An Initial meeting with the Heads of Technology and Employees who relied on the current FSS systems illuminated the following solution requirements:

“We work fast and loose”
“Collaborative learning is a big thing here”

Requirement: Solution should maintain Company culture & tempo.

“We need a better way to transfer knowledge”

Requirement: Solution should involve onboarding protocol.

“Some people don’t know how to manage documents”
“Every team has their own system”

Requirement: Solution should involve learning & protocol implementation.

“There’s redundancy in the folders”
“I don’t know where to look for what I need”

Requirement: Solution should incoorporate information architecture best practices.

3
HYPOTHESIS

If a lack of or variation in protocol is contributing to confusion amongst employees, a solution that outlines best practice guidelines while preserving the core work culture can reduce confusion by structuring the transition from start-up to large-scale company.

4
INVESTIGATION PLAN

  • Recruit Users (employees with varying frequency and style of FSS use)
  • User Interviews (understand current sentiments about the workflow & identify FSS pain points)
  • Task Analysis (observe & test employees from multiple workgroups to understand how each department organizes & uses the FSS)
  • Affinity Wall (synthesize findings from interviews and task analysis by grouping common themes between workgroups/employees)
  • Present Best Practice Solutions to stakeholders & employees

5
USER INTERVIEW & TASK OBSERVATION

Contextual inquiry uncovered:

  • Communication mediums & meeting strategies that were enjoyed by employees and should be leveraged into the solution.
  • Lack of consistency for onboarding & FSS training.
  • Different practices across workgroups for archiving files, onboarding new-hires, and naming files by convention.

6
AFFINITY DIAGRAMMING FOR SOLUTIONS

By creating an interactive affinity diagram, we were able to organize common concerns between workgroups and visualize potential solutions.
Our team identified and presented where new team-oriented or company-wide strategies were necessary to meet growth and employee needs. New work habits, organizational architecture, and technologies were reccomended based on our research findings. Implementation also acknowledged where company culture could be maintained and incoorporated. The following deliverables were presented to shareholders:

  • Organizational Restructuring
    Workgroups with overlapping responsibilities, frequent use of the same folders or creation of duplicate folders, and frequent communication were provided new new cross-comunication & workflow guidelines.
  • New Habits
    Workgroup habits such as FSS organization and file nameing convention often depended on team needs, such as the Product Development team which required letter combinations at the end of their CAD files to differentiate for manufacturing. By providing the guidelines for an "Organization Field Day", workgroups were able to adopt uniform practices for onboarding, documentation formatting, organization best practices, workshop stucure, naming conventions, organization checklists, QA protocols, administrator duties.
  • New Technology
    Competitive analysis of FSS & Remote Collaboration Tools: GDrive, SharePoint, OneDrive, DropBox Business, AirTable.FSS options were aso ranked based on: Cost, Scalability, Adjustment time, Difficulty of behavior change/Learning curve, Future Sustainability, Culture maintenance, Required effort, & Technical feasability.
